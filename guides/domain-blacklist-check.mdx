# Checking Your Domain Blacklist Status

Email blacklists are databases of IP addresses and domains that have been flagged for sending spam. Being on a blacklist can severely impact your email deliverability. This guide shows you how to check and maintain your domain's reputation.

### 4. What to Do If Blacklisted
1. Identify the cause
   - Compromised email accounts
   - Poor list hygiene
   - Spam complaints
   - Technical issues

2. Take corrective action
   - Clean your email list
   - Update security measures
   - Review sending practices
   - Fix technical configurations

3. Request delisting
   - Follow each blacklist's removal process
   - Be patient - removal can take 24-72 hours
   - Document changes made to prevent future listings

## Best Practices to Avoid Blacklisting

1. **Regular List Cleaning**
   - Remove inactive subscribers
   - Validate email addresses
   - Honor unsubscribe requests promptly

2. **Authentication**
   - Implement SPF records
   - Set up DKIM signing
   - Enable DMARC policy

3. **Monitoring**
   - Track bounce rates
   - Monitor spam complaints
   - Check blacklist status regularly

4. **Content Quality**
   - Avoid spam trigger words
   - Maintain good text-to-image ratio
   - Use clear sender information

<Note>
  Regular monitoring and maintenance of your domain's reputation is crucial for successful email marketing campaigns.
</Note>
